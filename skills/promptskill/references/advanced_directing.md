PART 2 — Advanced Cinematic Directing

 

STORY BEATS

Every scene should naturally progress.

Beginning. The audience immediately understands the situation.

Development. Something changes.

Reaction. Characters respond naturally.

Ending. Leave a memorable final visual.

Even a 5–10 second video should contain a complete emotional arc.

 

CAUSE AND EFFECT

Every movement has a reason.

Characters never move randomly.

Every glance... Every step... Every hand movement... Every facial change... must be motivated by something happening inside or outside the frame.

Visible consequences strengthen realism.

REAL-WORLD PHYSICS

Objects have believable weight.

Clothing reacts naturally.

Hair follows momentum.

Smoke disperses realistically.

Dust lingers.

Water splashes with mass.

Glass reflects correctly.

Light behaves naturally.

No floating objects unless intentionally supernatural.

 

CAMERA MOTIVATION

Camera movement always has purpose.

Never move randomly.

Follow emotion.

Reveal information.

Increase tension.

Create intimacy.

Build scale.

Guide viewer attention.

Every movement should feel like a professional cinematographer made the decision.

 

VISUAL HIERARCHY

The audience immediately knows where to look.

Use depth.

Foreground framing.

Leading lines.

Contrast.

Subject isolation.

Natural eye direction.

Balanced composition.

 

EMOTIONAL SUBTEXT

Do not state emotions.

Show them.

Fear becomes hesitation.

Confidence becomes relaxed posture.

Anger becomes restrained breathing.

Joy becomes genuine eye movement.

Sadness becomes slower motion and fading focus.

Everything is shown through performance.

 

CINEMATIC RHYTHM

Movement has pacing.

Alternate between stillness and motion.

Allow tiny pauses before important actions.

Natural anticipation improves realism.

Every movement has acceleration and deceleration.

Nothing starts or stops instantly.

 

FRAME QUALITY

Every frame should be capable of standing alone as a cinematic photograph.

Professional composition.

Natural perspective.

Authentic lighting.

Rich depth.

Film-quality texture.

Emotion visible without dialogue.

Maximum realism and immersion.

**Clean Frame — No Artificial Borders**  
The frame must always be clean. Never generate film reel borders, sprocket holes, stamp effects, decorative frames, or any border-like artifacts, even when the user requests vintage/retro/film looks. Vintage aesthetics are achieved only through grading, grain, and lens behavior.

**Consistent Color Science**  
All frames in a sequence must share the same color grading. If the user specifies a color grade, apply and repeat it exactly across every prompt. If not specified, establish one unified color palette at the beginning and maintain it strictly.

PART 4 — Shot Psychology & Visual Storytelling

 

CAMERA AS A STORYTELLER

The camera is an invisible character.

Every framing decision should communicate emotion, intention, power, vulnerability, or narrative progression.

Never choose a shot randomly.

 

SHOT SIZE

Extreme Close-Up Reveal tiny emotional changes. Eyes, fingertips, lips, sweat, tears.

Close-Up Create intimacy. Capture emotion before action.

Medium Shot Balance emotion and body language.

Medium Wide Show interaction between character and environment.

Wide Shot Reveal isolation, scale, danger, loneliness, freedom.

Extreme Wide Establish geography and emotional distance.

 

CAMERA HEIGHT

Eye Level Neutral observation.

Low Angle Power. Confidence. Dominance. Heroism.

High Angle Weakness. Exposure. Fear. Isolation.

Ground Level Increase realism and physical presence.

Overhead Create emotional detachment or reveal spatial relationships.

 

CAMERA DISTANCE

Closer camera creates emotional connection.

Farther camera creates emotional separation.

Move closer during emotional realization.

Move farther during loss, defeat, or loneliness.

 

CAMERA MOVEMENT

Static Calm. Control. Suspense.

Slow Push-In Build tension. Reveal realization. Increase intimacy.

Slow Pull-Back Create loneliness. Reveal consequences.

Tracking Follow determination. Persistence. Journey.

Handheld Urgency. Instability. Fear. Documentary realism.

Orbit Wonder. Reflection. Emotional transition.

Crane Reveal scale. Create cinematic spectacle.

 

VISUAL TENSION

Conflict should exist inside the frame.

Use empty space.

Blocked paths.

Doorways.

Shadows.

Reflections.

Foreground objects.

Natural obstacles.

Depth layers.

The frame itself should create emotional pressure.

 

CHARACTER POSITIONING

Center Frame Confidence. Importance.

Off-Center Uncertainty. Imbalance.

Small inside frame Isolation.

Large inside frame Power.

Hidden behind objects Mystery.

Silhouette Identity through shape.

DEPTH

Always create visual layers.

Foreground.

Subject.

Background.

Atmospheric separation.

Natural perspective.

Depth increases realism.

 

RHYTHM

Alternate stillness with motion.

Pause before important actions.

Allow anticipation.

Let reactions finish naturally.

Never rush emotional moments.

 

VISUAL PAYOFF

A final expression.

A revealing movement.

A symbolic object.

DIRECTOR'S PRINCIPLE

Every shot must answer three questions:

Why this framing?

Why this movement?

Why this moment?

If a shot cannot justify all three, simplify it.

The final result should feel intentionally directed, emotionally truthful, visually coherent, physically believable, and worthy of a premium feature film.

PART 7 — GROUNDED REALISM ENFORCEMENT PROTOCOL (MANDATORY)

This protocol is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Every prompt generated by this skill MUST enforce grounded realism. The output video must be indistinguishable from real footage shot by a real camera operator.

MOTION REALISM

All human motion must obey real-world physics:
- Weight, inertia, and momentum are always present
- Bodies accelerate and decelerate naturally — nothing starts or stops instantly
- Walking has heel-to-toe contact, arm counter-swing, and shoulder rotation
- Running has visible exertion: breathing, muscle tension, sweat
- Turning involves weight transfer and natural body rotation
- Sitting down and standing up involve center-of-gravity shifts
- Every action has preparation (wind-up) and follow-through

NEVER include in any prompt:
- Floaty, weightless, or gravity-defying movement (unless explicitly supernatural)
- Perfectly smooth, robotic, or mechanical human motion
- Instantaneous direction changes without deceleration
- Simultaneous synchronized movement of multiple characters

ALWAYS include in prompts:
- "natural human motion with visible weight and momentum"
- "organic body movement with subtle micro-adjustments"
- "physically grounded motion following real-world physics"

TEXTURE REALISM

Every surface in the prompt must be described with physical texture detail:

Human Skin:
- "natural skin texture with visible pores, subtle oil, micro-imperfections"
- "realistic subsurface scattering, natural skin color variation"
- "no plastic skin, no wax texture, no airbrushed smoothness"

Hair:
- "individual hair strands visible, natural flyaways, realistic volume"
- "hair responds to gravity and movement with natural weight"
- "no helmet hair, no blob texture, no uniform strands"

Fabric/Clothing:
- "visible fabric weave/fiber, natural wrinkles at joints and stress points"
- "gravity-responsive drape, material-appropriate behavior"
- "no painted-on clothing, no impossible smoothness"

Environment:
- "weathered surfaces with natural wear, dust, and age marks"
- "realistic material interaction with light (metal reflects, wood absorbs, glass refracts)"
- "no pristine, sterile, or overly clean surfaces unless story-motivated"

CAMERA REALISM

Every prompt must describe camera behavior as if operated by a real human:
- "natural handheld micro-movements, subtle operator breathing"
- "organic focus behavior with slight hunting before locking"
- "motivated camera movement — never random, never perfectly smooth"
- Small imperfections: tiny reframe corrections, slight overshoot, natural hesitation

NEVER describe camera as:
- Perfectly smooth gimbal (unless explicitly a steadicam shot)
- Floating or flying without physical motivation
- Teleporting between positions

HUMAN BEHAVIOR REALISM

Characters must behave like real people, not AI-generated mannequins:
- "natural blinking, micro-swallowing, subtle weight shifts"
- "breathing visible in chest/abdomen, rate changes with emotion"
- "idle fidgeting, tiny posture adjustments, natural eye saccades"
- "mixed emotions visible simultaneously (smile with tense jaw)"

NEVER allow:
- Perfectly still, statue-like characters
- Robotic or overly precise movements
- Dead, unfocused, or glassy eyes
- Unnaturally perfect posture

ANTI-AI ARTIFACT ENFORCEMENT

Every prompt MUST end with or integrate these quality anchors:
- "photorealistic, indistinguishable from real camera footage"
- "natural skin texture with visible pores and subsurface scattering"
- "organic human motion with physical weight and natural timing"
- "authentic camera behavior with subtle operator imperfections"
- "real-world physics governing all motion, cloth, hair, and environmental elements"

NEVER include these AI-bait words (they produce artificial results):
- "beautiful" (replace with specific observable qualities)
- "perfect" (replace with "natural" or "authentic")
- "stunning" (replace with specific visual descriptions)
- "masterpiece" (replace with technical quality descriptors)
- "8K, ultra HD" (replace with "feature film quality" or "photorealistic")

GROUNDED PRIORITY RULE

When any stylistic request conflicts with physical realism:

Priority: Physics > Realism > Story > Style

- Dream sequences → achieved through lighting, color grading, slow motion, and atmosphere — NOT through breaking physics
- Surreal requests → grounded interpretation with motivated visual effects — NOT floaty or plastic motion
- Stylized requests → apply style through color science, lens choice, and composition — NOT through artificial motion or texture
- ONLY explicit "anime style" request bypasses grounded enforcement entirely

This protocol ensures every prompt produces video that a viewer would believe was shot with a real camera, capturing real people, in a real location.
